The biggest fear that exists today when placing implants is how long that implant will last in the mouth. We have had the false belief that peri-implant bone loss is inevitable. The etiology of peri-implantitis has been extensively studied in the last two decades, and we know with certainty that it is multifactorial and we also know that the main cause is bacterial leakage which is closely linked to the micro-movement of the prosthetic-implant connection.

Many studies carried out have shown that the Morse Cone Connection is capable of significantly reducing this bacterial micro-leakage, with a significant reduction in peri-crestal bone resorption and a marked improvement in the health of the peri-crestal soft tissues, the latter being the key and the long-term success of implants and their restorations.

Other topics to be discussed, all of daily practical interest to the clinician, are the use of water laser technology in the treatment of bone and soft tissue to prevent diseases such as peri-implantitis and prolong the health of gingival tissue.

These topics (Morse Cone Connection, Tissue Healing Concept and Conometry) will be discussed in depth by international speakers at this event to be held on April 22, 2023 in Doral-FL.
